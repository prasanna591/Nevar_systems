Built for Greater Possibilities

Photon VTOL combines the vertical take-off and landing capability of a multirotor with the speed and efficiency of a fixed-wing aircraft. It is engineered to cover large project areas, operate from confined locations and carry multiple professional payloads[span_6](start_span)[span_6](end_span). From high-accuracy mapping to real-time surveillance, Photon helps teams complete demanding missions with greater efficiency[span_7](start_span)[span_7](end_span).

Vertical Take-off. Fixed-Wing Performance.

Photon takes off vertically, transitions smoothly into efficient forward flight and lands vertically at the mission location[span_8](start_span)[span_8](end_span). No runway, launcher or complex recovery system is required[span_9](start_span)[span_9](end_span). This makes Photon suitable for deployment in forests, agricultural fields, mining sites, infrastructure corridors and remote terrain[span_10](start_span)[span_10](end_span).

Precision in Every Mission

Photon supports GNSS with PPK positioning for improved geospatial accuracy and consistent aerial data collection[span_11](start_span)[span_11](end_span). Its autonomous mission capability enables precise take-off, flight-path execution, data capture and vertical landing with minimal operator intervention[span_12](start_span)[span_12](end_span).

110 Minutes of Endurance

Stay in the air. Cover more ground. At an operating altitude of 120 metres, it can map approximately 4–5 km² in a single flight, reducing repeated launches, battery changes and overall field time[span_13](start_span)[span_13](end_span).

One Aircraft. Multiple Missions.

Photon features a modular payload system that allows operators to configure the aircraft according to different project requirements[span_28](start_span)[span_28](end_span). Payloads can be quickly interchanged, enabling a single aircraft to support mapping, inspection, monitoring and surveillance operations[span_29](start_span)[span_29](end_span).
